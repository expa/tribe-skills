# Tribe Design — Voice

How Tribe AI writes. Use for ANY copy in a Tribe artifact — website sections,
social cards, slides, documents, articles. Two registers share one DNA:
**website voice** (headlines, section copy, CTAs, microcopy) and **article
voice** (long-form editorial). Pick the register, then write.

Distilled from all published articles and the live site copy —
`references/voice-analysis.md` has the full analysis with verbatim examples;
read it before writing anything longer than a headline.

## Shared DNA (always true)

1. **The people thesis.** Models are commodity; humans — judgment, talent,
   deployment — are the unlock. "AI doesn't transform a company. People do."
2. **Production is the finish line.** Never "strategy", "insights", or
   "enablement" — software running in production and staying there.
3. **Contrast construction** — the signature move: "isn't X — it's Y",
   "tools, not results", "practitioners not consultants".
4. **Specificity over adjectives.** Numbers, named systems, real timelines
   persuade; superlatives are absent.
5. **Confidence without hype.** Declarative sentences, short beats, no
   hedging — and no hype vocabulary. An experienced operator, calm because
   they've done it.

**Vocabulary they use:** production, ship, deploy, forward-deployed, embed,
build/builders, frontier, compound, unlock (always with a concrete object),
real, own it end to end, judgment, institutional knowledge, talent, the bar.

**Never:** cutting-edge, revolutionary, game-changing, seamless, best-in-class,
world-class, empower, harness, journey, synergy, digital transformation,
"AI-powered solutions", delve, "AI landscape", "in today's world", paradigm
shift, utilize. No fear-mongering about AI replacing people. Minimal hedging
("arguably", "perhaps" essentially never). Say "builders", never "resources"
or "consultants".

## Register A — Website voice

- **Headlines: sentence case, 3–9 words, terminal period. Always.**
  "It takes a Tribe." / "Build at the frontier." Two structures dominate:
  staccato triad ("We embed. We build. We don't stop until it's in
  production.") and contrast pivot ("Most companies have the tools, not the
  results.").
- **Eyebrows**: short plain noun phrases, no punctuation — "Insights",
  "How we work". The headline carries the personality, not the eyebrow.
- **Body**: 1–3 sentences per block, ≤ ~20 words each. Em dash as the
  mid-sentence pivot that lands the point. Pattern: *we do X → you get Y*.
- **CTAs are invitations**: "Start a conversation", "Join us", "Come build
  with us" — never "Book a demo", "Get started free", or "Learn more" as
  primary.
- **No** exclamation marks, emoji, question headlines, or Title Case.
  Playfulness only in low-stakes microcopy (404, hiring steps).

Rewrite check — generic: "We leverage cutting-edge AI solutions to drive
digital transformation." → Tribe: "Every enterprise has access to the same
models. We build the systems — and the teams — that turn them into
production. That's the difference."

## Register B — Article voice

- **Thesis-first.** The argument or a scene arrives in sentence one — never
  "In recent years…". Research posts may open with a literal "## TL;DR".
- **Operator's evidence.** Every claim gets a number, a named system, or a
  first-hand scene, with sources for external stats. "63% of people who built
  working AI tools had never deployed anything beyond their own laptop."
- **Confession over hedging.** Certainty about the thesis, candor about
  mistakes: "We underestimated how hard it would be to make companies care."
- **Named ideas.** Coin a handle for the finding ("the 80% cliff") so it
  travels.
- **Beat-drop rhythm.** Long passages resolve to 1–3-word sentences:
  "That's the bar." / "No vibes." Paragraphs 2–4 sentences; single-sentence
  paragraphs as beats.
- **Formatting**: numbered frameworks, bulleted lists with **bold lead-ins**
  followed by an em dash, pull-quotes for findings and customer voices.
- **Clients anonymized but vivid** ("a global consulting firm renowned for
  its leadership in corporate turnarounds"); partners (OpenAI, Anthropic,
  AWS) named proudly.
- **Closings**: compressed restatement + direct invitation. "That's the
  unlock. That's Tribe." Articles allow a rare "we're hiring!" and the 👉 CTA
  pointer; announcements may sign off personally ("— Jackie & Noah").
- **Titles by category**: Announcements — plain declarative / founder-personal
  ("Why we bought a recruiting firm"); Research — the finding as a sentence
  ("We measured every person at our company on AI. Here's what we learned.");
  technical guides — Topic: Benefit colon titles in Title Case.

## Register differences at a glance

| | Website | Article |
|---|---|---|
| Shape | compress to aphorism | expand with evidence |
| Person | corporate we + you | allows "I", first names |
| Exclamation / emoji | never | rare "!"; 👉 for CTAs |
| Humor | microcopy only | dry asides in-argument |
| Tense | timeless present | anchored in moments |
| Headings | sentence case + period | thesis fragments or functional Title Case |
